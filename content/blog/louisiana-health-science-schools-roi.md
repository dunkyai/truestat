## Louisiana Has a College ROI Problem

Louisiana's average college ROI score is 33.2 out of 100. Across 53 scored institutions, that puts it near the bottom nationally. Most students in the state are paying for degrees that underdeliver on earnings.

But four schools are breaking the pattern — and three of them aren't the ones you'd expect.

## The Top 4, by the Numbers

| School | ROI Score | 10-Year Median Earnings | Median Debt | Debt-to-Income |
|--------|-----------|------------------------|-------------|----------------|
| LSU Health Sciences Center–New Orleans | 65 | $78,495 | $19,500 | 0.25 |
| Baton Rouge General Medical Center School of Nursing | 64 | $75,071 | $16,750 | 0.22 |
| ITI Technical College | 62 | $68,342 | $15,006 | 0.22 |
| Tulane University of Louisiana | 61 | $63,268 | $20,500 | 0.32 |

The state average ROI is 33.2. These four schools nearly double it. LSU Health Sciences Center leads at 65 — almost twice the state mean.

## Tulane Gets the Attention. These Schools Get the Results.

Tulane is the name brand. It's the school people outside Louisiana know. And it does perform well — ROI of 61, 10-year earnings of $63,268, an 87.5% graduation rate.

But LSU Health Sciences Center graduates earn $78,495 at the ten-year mark. That's $15,227 more than Tulane grads, with lower debt ($19,500 vs. $20,500) and a tighter debt-to-income ratio (0.25 vs. 0.32).

Baton Rouge General's nursing program tells a similar story: $75,071 in 10-year earnings with just $16,750 in median debt. That's a debt-to-income ratio of 0.22 — the lowest among these four.

## The Technical College That Outperforms Most of the State

ITI Technical College in Baton Rouge scores a 62. Its graduates earn $68,342 at ten years with $15,006 in debt. For a technical program with a net price of $20,850, that's a strong return.

Compare that to the state's flagship, LSU A&M, which scores 56 with 10-year earnings of $61,251 and $20,500 in median debt. ITI's graduates carry less debt, earn more, and don't need four years to get there.

## What the Data Says

Three patterns emerge:

**1. Health science training pays in Louisiana.** The top three schools by ROI are all health-focused. Nursing, radiologic technology, and medical sciences produce graduates with earnings well above the state median.

**2. Brand name isn't everything.** Baton Rouge General Medical Center School of Nursing isn't in anyone's college rankings. It doesn't have a football team. Its graduates earn $75,071 a year ten years out.

**3. Low debt matters as much as high earnings.** Every school in this top four keeps median debt under $21,000. ITI and Baton Rouge General keep it under $17,000. The debt-to-income ratios (0.22–0.32) are well below the national danger zone.

## The Bottom Line

Louisiana ranks near the bottom nationally for college ROI. But the state has a quiet pipeline of health science and technical programs producing graduates who out-earn most of the country's college grads. If you're choosing a school in Louisiana, the data says to look beyond the brand names — the nursing programs and technical colleges are where the returns are.
