## ROI 76 at $15,678 a Year. That's UMD-College Park.

Johns Hopkins scores an 84 on our ROI index. It gets the magazine covers, the brand recognition, the "dream school" label. No argument there.

But University of Maryland-College Park scores 76 — 90% of the outcome — at $15,678 per year in-state. Its graduates earn $82,860 at the ten-year mark. And it's not alone. Three more Maryland public schools crack ROI 55 at under $18,000 per year.

Maryland's public university system is one of the best-kept secrets in college ROI.

## The Maryland Public ROI Ladder

| School | ROI Score | Net Price (In-State) | 10-Year Earnings |
|---|---|---|---|
| **University of Maryland-College Park** | **76** | **$15,678** | **$82,860** |
| University of Maryland-Baltimore County | 63 | $16,467 | $69,960 |
| Towson University | 59 | $17,413 | $64,390 |
| Salisbury University | 55 | $17,743 | $61,515 |

All four schools cost under $18,000 per year. All four score above 55 on our ROI index. The price spread between them is just $2,065 — but UMD-College Park's graduates earn $21,345 more than Salisbury's at the ten-year mark.

## The Public-Private Gap That Barely Exists

In most states, private schools dominate ROI rankings. Massachusetts is the textbook example: its private schools outscore publics by 12 points on average.

Maryland is different. Across 52 schools in our dataset:

| Category | Average ROI |
|---|---|
| Maryland Public Schools | 44.0 |
| Maryland Private Schools | 45.0 |
| **Gap** | **1.0 point** |

A one-point gap. Statistically negligible. Maryland families choosing between public and private aren't making a meaningful ROI trade-off — they're mostly choosing how much to pay for a comparable outcome.

## Johns Hopkins vs. UMD-College Park

The comparison is unavoidable. Johns Hopkins is Maryland's crown jewel — ROI 84, a globally recognized research university, and 10-year earnings that reflect it.

But Hopkins doesn't report net price data in the same way, and private university sticker prices in its tier typically run $55,000–$65,000 per year before aid. Even with generous financial aid, most families pay significantly more than $15,678.

UMD-College Park at ROI 76 and $15,678 per year delivers the bulk of the earnings upside at a fraction of the cost. For families where price matters — and it almost always does — the math favors College Park.

## Why Maryland Publics Perform This Well

Three factors stand out:

**1. Proximity to Washington, D.C.** UMD-College Park is 8 miles from the Capitol. UMBC is 40 miles. Towson is 45 miles. Graduates walk into one of the highest-paying labor markets in the country — federal government, defense contracting, consulting, cybersecurity, biotech.

**2. Strong STEM and professional programs.** Maryland's flagship campuses have invested heavily in computer science, engineering, and business programs that feed directly into D.C.-corridor employers.

**3. In-state tuition that hasn't spiraled.** All four schools remain under $18,000 per year in-state. That keeps debt loads manageable and ROI scores high.

## Bottom Line

Johns Hopkins earns its reputation. But for Maryland families weighing cost against outcome, the public system delivers exceptional value. UMD-College Park at $15,678 and ROI 76 is arguably the best deal on the Eastern seaboard. UMBC, Towson, and Salisbury round out a public lineup that most states can't match.

The data is clear: in Maryland, you don't have to pay private-school prices to get private-school results.
