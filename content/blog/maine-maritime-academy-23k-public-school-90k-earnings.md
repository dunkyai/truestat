## $89,964 at Ten Years. From a School Most People Have Never Heard Of.

Maine Maritime Academy in Castine, Maine charges $23,414 per year in-state. Its graduates report median earnings of $89,964 ten years after enrollment. That is the highest 10-year earnings figure of any public school in the state — by a wide margin.

The next closest public school, University of Southern Maine, produces graduates earning $49,958. The state flagship, University of Maine, comes in at $48,653. Maine Maritime's graduates earn nearly double what the average Maine public school graduate earns.

## The Numbers

| Metric | Maine Maritime Academy |
|---|---|
| ROI Score | 71 / 100 |
| Net Price (In-State) | $23,414 |
| Median Earnings (10yr) | $89,964 |
| Median Earnings (6yr) | $93,944 |
| Median Debt | $27,000 |
| Debt-to-Income Ratio | 0.30 |
| Graduation Rate | 59.9% |

The debt-to-income ratio of 0.30 means graduates carry roughly 30 cents of debt for every dollar they earn. That is well within manageable territory.

One unusual detail: 6-year earnings ($93,944) are actually higher than 10-year earnings ($89,964). This likely reflects the maritime industry's structure — graduates step into high-paying licensed officer positions immediately, and the credential premium is front-loaded.

## How It Stacks Up Against Maine's Best

Maine Maritime doesn't just beat other public schools. It beats most of the state's elite private colleges on raw earnings.

| School | Type | 10yr Earnings | ROI Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Maine Maritime Academy** | **Public** | **$89,964** | **71** |
| Bowdoin College | Private | $82,735 | 77 |
| Colby College | Private | $80,490 | 74 |
| Bates College | Private | $69,498 | 70 |
| University of Maine | Public | $48,653 | 38 |
| University of Southern Maine | Public | $49,958 | 42 |

Bowdoin and Colby post higher overall ROI scores because their graduation rates exceed 90%, compared to Maritime's 60%. But on the metric that matters most to your bank account — how much you earn — Maritime wins.

## Why Maritime Academies Are a Blind Spot

There are seven state maritime academies in the U.S. They train students for careers as licensed merchant marine officers, marine engineers, and logistics professionals. These are specialized, credential-driven programs that funnel graduates directly into high-paying roles in shipping, energy, and defense.

Maritime academies rarely appear in mainstream college rankings. They are small, hyper-specialized, and don't fit the liberal arts mold that dominates higher education media. But for students who want a clear path from degree to career, the ROI case is hard to argue with.

$23,414 in. $89,964 out. That is the kind of return most private colleges can't match at three times the price.

## The Graduation Rate Question

Maritime's 59.9% graduation rate is a real weakness. Nearly four in ten students who start don't finish. The program is demanding — part military structure, part engineering school, part at-sea training. It is not for everyone.

But for the students who complete it, the payoff is clear. And the $27,000 median debt load means even students who leave aren't buried in loans the way they might be at a costlier institution.

## Bottom Line

Maine Maritime Academy is a case study in what specialized public education can do. It outearns Bowdoin. It outearns Colby. It costs a fraction of either. If you are a Maine student — or any student — willing to pursue a maritime career, the data says this is one of the strongest ROI plays in New England.

The ROI Score of 71 puts it 27 points above the Maine state average of 43.8. No other public school in the state is even close.
