## ROI 56 at $10,074 a Year. That's Manhattan Area Technical College.

Iowa State University scores ROI 57. It costs $18,589 per year in-state. Graduates carry $22,869 in median debt.

Manhattan Area Technical College in Kansas scores ROI 56 — one point lower — at $10,074 per year. Its graduates carry $9,500 in debt. That's 46% cheaper tuition and 58% less debt for nearly identical ROI.

This isn't a fluke. Five Kansas technical and community colleges score ROI 51–56, rivaling or matching Big Ten flagships at a fraction of the price.

Kansas's flagships help — University of Kansas and Kansas State both score 52–56 — but the technical colleges are the real engine of that gap.

## Why Kansas Technical Colleges Punch Above Their Weight

Three factors:

**1. Workforce-aligned programs.** These aren't general-education holding pens. Manhattan Area Tech, Flint Hills Tech, and Salina Area Tech run targeted programs in welding, HVAC, nursing, and industrial technology that connect directly to employer demand in the region.

**2. Ultra-low debt loads.** When your graduates owe $6,500–$10,000 instead of $22,000, the ROI math shifts dramatically. Even moderate earnings produce strong returns against a small denominator.

**3. Fast completion times.** Many technical college programs run 12–24 months. Students enter the labor market years before their four-year peers, earning while others are still accumulating debt.

## Bottom Line

The best-value colleges in America aren't where most families look. Five Kansas technical colleges deliver ROI scores of 51–56 — the same range as Big Ten flagships — at 30–50% of the cost and with a fraction of the debt.

Manhattan Area Technical College at $10,074 and ROI 56 effectively matches Iowa State at $18,589. Pratt Community College at $9,731 and ROI 53 gets 91% of the University of Iowa's ROI outcome at 43% of the price.

Kansas is a hidden-gem state for college value. The data says so.
